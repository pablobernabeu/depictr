# Simulated lexical-decision experiment

A reproducibly simulated psycholinguistic lexical-decision experiment,
in which participants judge whether letter strings are real words. The
crossed participant and item structure makes it a natural fit for
mixed-effects models, and hence for
[`optimizer_fixef_plot()`](https://pablobernabeu.github.io/depictr/reference/optimizer_fixef_plot.md),
[`coefficient_plot()`](https://pablobernabeu.github.io/depictr/reference/coefficient_plot.md)
and the distribution plots.

## Usage

``` r
lexical_decision
```

## Format

A data frame with 960 rows and 7 variables:

- participant:

  Participant identifier (factor, 24 levels).

- item:

  Item identifier (factor, 40 levels).

- condition:

  Priming condition: `related` or `unrelated`.

- modality:

  Presentation modality: `visual` or `auditory`.

- word_frequency:

  Word frequency on the Zipf scale.

- RT:

  Reaction time, in milliseconds.

- accuracy:

  Response accuracy: 1 correct, 0 incorrect.

## Source

Simulated; see `data-raw/generate_datasets.R`.

## Details

The data are synthetic, generated by `data-raw/generate_datasets.R` with
a fixed seed; they do not describe any real individuals.
